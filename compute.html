Compute Futures

A futures contract for inference — priced by energy expenditure, grounded in entropy, settled on-chain.

What is it

A financial instrument that lets agents and enterprises acquire inference compute at a known cost — derived from the thermodynamic work required to produce it, not speculative market pricing.

01

Energy-Priced

Contract pricing reflects the real energy cost of inference — joules in, tokens out. No abstraction layers between compute and value.

02

Entropy-Settled

Settlement is anchored to provable entropy minimization on-chain, giving both counterparties a physical basis for contract fulfillment.

03

Agent-Ready

Designed for autonomous agents that need to acquire, budget, and allocate inference resources programmatically at scale.

How it works

The contract lifecycle connects thermodynamic reality to financial settlement.

Pricing

Contracts are priced using the thermoeconomic cost function — mapping energy input and entropy output to a fair market rate for inference compute per unit of work.

Acquisition

Agents acquire futures positions denominated in Qi, locking in inference capacity at a known energy-equivalent cost for a defined time horizon.

Settlement

At expiry, contracts settle against on-chain entropy metrics — the provable thermodynamic work delivered by the network during the contract period.
